<p>Ander Wilson is Assistant Professor in the <a href="http://www.stat.colostate.edu/">Department of Statistics</a> at <a href="https://www.colostate.edu/">Colorado State University</a>. 
He is a faculty member in the <a href="https://www.research.colostate.edu/pach/">Partnership on Air Quality Climate and Health (PACH)</a> at CSU and organizes the <a href="https://envbiostat-csu.github.io">environmental biostatistics working group</a> at CSU.</p>

<p>Ander received his PhD from the <a href="http://www.stat.ncsu.edu/">Department of Statistics</a> at North Carolina State University. 
During that time, he was an ORISE fellow at the US Environmental Protection Agency. 
He was a Postdoctoral Research Fellow in the <a href="https://www.hsph.harvard.edu/biostatistics/">Department of Biostatistics</a> at the Harvard T.H. Chan School of Public Health. 
Ander received a BA in mathematics from the <a href="https://www.uvm.edu/cems/mathstat">University of Vermont</a> and previously worked at <a href="https://mathematica-mpr.com/">Mathematica Policy Research</a>.</p>

<p>Ander research program focuses on developing statistical methods for environmental health research. Current areas of research includes:</p>
<ul>
  <li>Health effects of exposure to environmental mixtures</li>
  <li>Effect heterogeneity</li>
  <li>Health effects of maternal exposure to chemicals on birth and childrenâ€™s health outcomes</li>
  <li>Bayesian methods</li>
  <li>Machine learning</li>
  <li>Health effects of wildfire smoke exposure</li>
</ul>
